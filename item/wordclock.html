WORDCLOCK

Project details

Instead of using numbers to tell time, the wordclock uses words to represent time. The clock itself runs off of an fpga board powered by a usb charger and about 90 LEDs. Similar to digital clocks, the wordclock features an alarm, time setting, brightness control (and down timer to automatically dim during night), and a unique pulsating light option for when solid lit up words are too boring.

The project itself is inspired off another individuals wordclock, my interest in making my own, and learning about LED matrices. Unfortunately, due to time restraints I took an easier path of wiring the words in parallel. Overall, creating the wordclock gave an excellent introduction to hardware programming around an fpga board using verilog, PWMs, and also aquainted me with simple wiring.
